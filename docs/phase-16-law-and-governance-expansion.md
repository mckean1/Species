# Phase 16: Law and Governance Expansion

Phase 16 deepens polity governance without changing the canonical monthly flow, Chronicle-first presentation model, or the existing settlement, material, and knowledge layers.

## What Changed

- Laws are now a smaller set of broader structural choices instead of a long pool of narrow one-off measures.
- Government form now contributes explicit baselines for legitimacy, cohesion, authority, centralization bias, and shared-governance bias.
- Polities now carry a lightweight governance state:
  - legitimacy
  - cohesion
  - authority
  - governability
  - peripheral strain
- Enacted laws now land unevenly through core effectiveness, peripheral effectiveness, and resistance instead of applying with identical force everywhere.
- Political bloc pressure now reacts more directly to governance state, material strain, settlement spread, and current law direction.

## Structural Law Set

The current law layer is intentionally small and high-level:

- `Centralize Stores`
- `Affirm Local Store Autonomy`
- `Impose Extraction Obligation`
- `Ease Extraction Burden`
- `Restrict Crisis Movement`
- `Protect Open Movement`
- `Strengthen Central Authority`
- `Affirm Shared Governance`
- `Adopt Frontier Integration Charter`

These laws are meant to shape how a polity coordinates survival, extraction, settlement, and order rather than provide a large catalog of minor modifiers.

## Governance Rules

- Proposal pressure now comes from real polity conditions such as shortages, deficits, migration stress, threat pressure, peripheral strain, and weak legitimacy/cohesion/authority.
- Government form affects proposal weighting and governability through stable baselines rather than flavor-only labels.
- Legitimacy is influenced by shortages, bloc dissatisfaction, local strain, and whether laws feel shared or imposed.
- Cohesion is influenced by migration stress, peripheral strain, settlement continuity, and integration/shared-governance posture.
- Authority is influenced by crisis, centralization, and whether the polity can still command acceptance.
- Governability is a compact aggregate of legitimacy, cohesion, authority, and peripheral strain.

## Compliance and Effective Landing

- Enacted laws now track:
  - enforcement strength
  - compliance level
  - core effectiveness
  - peripheral effectiveness
  - resistance level
- Core regions usually feel centralized or harsh laws more strongly.
- Peripheral settlements and presences comply less effectively when legitimacy, cohesion, or material conditions are weak.
- This remains aggregate. There is still no province-level bureaucracy or detailed administrative simulation.

## System Connections

- Material extraction and store redistribution now react to governance laws such as centralization, extraction burden, and frontier integration.
- Group pressure updates now apply law effects with different practical strength in core versus peripheral space.
- Political blocs now evaluate the current order using governance state and broad law direction.
- Chronicle law lines now report governance outcomes in more causal, player-facing language.

## Scope Guardrails

This is still not:

- a diplomacy overhaul
- a warfare overhaul
- a state office-holder simulation
- a detailed bureaucracy sim
- a culture / religion / language system
- an empire-scaling phase

The governance layer remains intentionally aggregate, explanatory, and suitable for later expansion.
